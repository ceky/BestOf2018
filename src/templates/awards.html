THE

BEST OF 2018

Best books

  • The Essex Serpent
    by Sarah Perry

    Set in London and an Essex village in the 1890's the novel has a great plot and develops the characters really well. I also loved the themes that appear in the novel and the way they are combined: science vs faith, friendship vs love, feminism, medicine and much more.

  • Love's Executioner and Other Tales of Psychotherapy
    by Irvin D. Yalom

    A collection of ten tales by psychotherapist Irvin D. Yalom and his pacients. The subject in the ten tales vary a lot and may affect the reader in different ways based on the experiences each of us lived.

  • Blindness
    by José Saramago

    Haven't really read much dystopian books this one was a really page turner, despite the writing style that is not really friendly. A city hit by a blindness epidemic and before we know, we are immersed in a world of hopelessness, violence and hate.

  • One Hundred Years of Solitude
    by Gabriel García Márquez

    The story walks us through seven generations of the Buendia family. Taking events from ordinary life and inserting elements of fantasy this book is one of the pioneers of magical realism.

  • Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind
    by Yuval Noah Harari

    This book covers a lot of knowledge related to the history of humankind. I would have loved to have a book like this in the high school, cause most certainly my passion for reading and history would have been much higher.

Best movies

  • Human Planet
    Year: 2011

    Wonderful! A TV series composed of 8 episodes that explores the life of humans in the most extreme conditions. The movie takes you on a journey in every possible habitat, where surviving seems impossible, but somehow the man adapted to the conditions.

  • Bohemian Rhapsody
    Year: 2018

    There are not many movies that I go to the theather to watch, but I'm so glad I did see the movie there. When the movie ended I had the exact same feeling after a live concert.

  • Benny & Joon
    Year: 1993

    A little love story between a mentally ill young woman and a crazy man played brilliantly by Johnny Depp. I had a really good time watching this. Oh, and the song from the beginning of the movie is so awesome: I'm Gonna Be (500 Miles)

  • Starship Troopers
    Year: 1997

    I watched this movie while visiting my friends in Spain. They described it to me as a combination of teenage romantic movie with an interstellar battle: mankind against a killer specie of insects. I couldn't resist it.

  • Fried Green Tomatoes
    Year: 1991

    A gifted group of actors delivering a good drama where friendship is celebrated. Not everyone's cup of tea, but I really loved the atmosphere in the movie. So 90's, so good.
